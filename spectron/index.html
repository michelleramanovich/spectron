We present Spectron, a novel approach to adapting pre-trained language models (LMs) to perform speech continuation. By leveraging pre-trained speech encoders, our model generates both text and speech outputs with the entire system being trained end-to-end operating directly on spectrograms. Training the entire model in the spectrogram domain simplifies our speech continuation system versus existing cascade methods which use discrete speech representations. We further show our method surpasses existing spoken language models both in semantic content and speaker preservation while also benefiting from the knowledge transferred from pre-existing models.

Model

The initial audio segment, labeled "Prompt," features the first 3 seconds of an original audio prompt extracted from the LibriSpeech test-clean test set. In the subsequent column, labeled "Generated Continuation," the audio clip features the continuation generated by our language model. The pre-trained LM is a 350M parameter model.
